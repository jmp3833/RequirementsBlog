Blog Week 2

For this first week, our team will be making a few alterations to the standard blog format for this course, mainly because we have not begun much work on our project yet. We have spent some time defining the project and setting up some of our goals that we would like to accomplish over the upcoming semester. The project we have decided to take on is a system that a team member has personally been working alongside their co op to accomplish on a part time basis. This project mainly revolves around a system that would be used to check the relative 'health' of many web and data services that currently exist in the company's infratructure. Determining if a specific service is 'healthy' will be done on an individual basis. These evaluations can range from sending simple JSON payloads to the API of a data service and checking for valid results, to making sure that notifications are properly sent out to customers in a timely manner. This system will be modular enough to be added to by any project team at the company who would like to have a simple way to verify that their data service is always functioning appropriately. The app will have several ways to display information about various applications to anyone in the company who would find the information relevant, including a system of logging, a frontend user interface that is updated every 60 seconds, and a notification system that will send emails, text messages, or pages to users who may need to verify 100% uptime of their program or service. There will be many requirements regarding the security, responsiveness, and uptime of the application that are yet to be determined. In order to better understand the scope of this project and information on the high level requirements, we have decided to generate a problem and vision statement in relation to the project:

Problem Statement

The problem of: Many developers and quality assurance engineers getting upset when they cannot develop or test code because they are not getting appropriate results upon deployment. Most of the time it turns out that a particular service that they rely on is having errors or is down completely and they have no way of know what specific service is down.
This affects quality assurance engineers, developers, managers, Team leaders, and legal/sales departments of the organization, the impact of which is inconvenience and increased development and debugging time. A successful solution would provide a clean interface that could alert QA engineers and developers when a service is down. This service could also be extended to have a web application that allows these stakeholders to check the status of all of the data services at any given time. This would drastically reduce the amount of time spent debugging new applications.

Vision Statement

For developers and quality assurance engineers that need to be aware of specific data services that are behaving incorrectly or down all together, an application will exist that can alert users via email,sms, or pager that the service has gone or behaved not as expected. There will also be a front end user interface and extensive logging that can be checked at any time to see what specific services are down or not behaving as expected.
